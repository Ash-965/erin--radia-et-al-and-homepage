Clare Johnson, Partnerships and Outreach Manager
University of South Wales


Founder of Women In Cyber Wales in 2018, Clare is passionate about inspiring young people, particularly girls to consider careers in cyber security and STEM subjects.
She has worked with schools, in the community and even with the Brownies and Guides to deliver Cyber Security days. Having worked with people as young as 5 via her outreach programme, Clare wants to encourage young people to be interest in technology but to also make them think about what goes on behind the technology to make it work.






Clare describes Cyber Security as 'one of the most exciting, varied and challenging industries' in her article, It was never a dress. The importance of diversity in cyber security

Here she talks about attending a meeting early on in a senior position in cyber security and how there was only one other female present and her role was the minute taker.
Within this she talks of the belitting behaviour and assumptions made by others within meetings.
However she states, "Women aren't neccessarily better, but we are different... We all bring something different to the party."
