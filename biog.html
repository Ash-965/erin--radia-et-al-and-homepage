Radia Perlman

Roots and Branches

Radia Perlman was born in Portsmouth, Virginia in 1951. In 1976 she graduated with a Masters in Mathematics from MIT. In 1988 she completed her PhD in Computer Science at the same institution, writing her thesis on computer networks that would be resistent to malfunction.

Working for Digital Equipment Corp in the 1980s, Radia developed a protocol that allowed bridges within a Local Area Network to communicate with one another, without getting stuck in redundant loops. She named this innovation the Spanning Tree Protcol. It has shaped the way we interact with the world online ever since, and earned Radia a nick-name that she would rather forget, Mother of the Internet. In 2004, building on her original invention, she devised Transparent Interconnection of Lots of Links, more commonly known as TRILL.

Radia Perlman continues to be a leading authority in compter science and who has won many awards for her work. She was inducted into the Internet Hall of Fame in 2014, and is currently a Fellow of Dell Technologies.
